================================================================================
  FUNDING MODEL, LEGAL SCOPE AND MERCADOPAGO HANDLING
  Platform: ProyectoHornero (Argentina only, UBA tesis project)
================================================================================

Companion document to "creator-money-reception-requirements.txt".
This file fixes the project's architectural and legal scope so the team can
defend it in front of the tesis panel and refer back to it during build.


================================================================================
1. CHOSEN FUNDING MODEL: Idea.me-style ("all-or-nothing", platform-held)
================================================================================

ProyectoHornero implements the "Idea.me-style" crowdfunding model — also used
by Kickstarter, Verkami, etc. — characterised by:

  - All-or-nothing campaign goals: contributions are only released to the
    creator if the campaign reaches its declared goal by the deadline.
  - Platform-side parking: while a campaign is still active (or pending
    success), donor money sits in the platform's MercadoPago account, not in
    the creator's account.
  - Refund on failure: if a campaign ends FAILED or CANCELLED, the platform
    issues refunds to each donor's original payment method.
  - Single payout on success: when a campaign ends SUCCESSFUL, the platform
    deducts its fee and transfers the net amount to the creator's CBU/CVU.

This model is the heavier of the two common Argentine patterns. The lighter
one (Donacionar-style, donor pays creator directly with no platform custody)
was evaluated and discarded because it cannot offer donor protection if a
campaign fails to reach its goal, which is a core value of the platform.


================================================================================
2. END-TO-END FLOW
================================================================================

  Step 1 — Creator publishes campaign
    Goal amount, deadline, description, and reward (if any) are declared.
    Creator must have completed verification (see creator-money-reception
    -requirements.txt) before publishing.

  Step 2 — Donor decides to contribute
    Donor selects amount, sees a one-screen form: amount + optional reward
    selection + email + Terms acceptance. Within tesis scope donors are
    always consumidor final (see §5), so no CUIT or fiscal data is asked.

  Step 3 — Platform creates Contribution (PENDING) and initiates MP Payment
    The MP Payment Brick (cards) is loaded in the browser. MP tokenizes the
    card; the backend calls MP /v1/payments with the platform's access token.
    Money is charged to the donor's card and credited to the platform's MP
    account (held balance).

  Step 4 — Contribution becomes APPROVED
    Platform updates the campaign's current_amount. The campaign progresses
    toward its goal.

  Step 5a — Campaign ends SUCCESSFUL (goal reached on or before deadline)
    Platform calculates: gross = sum(APPROVED contributions),
                         net   = gross - platform_fee - provider_fee
    Platform calls MP's payout API (or MP Money Out) to send `net` to the
    creator's CBU/CVU. Status moves to COMPLETED.

  Step 5b — Campaign ends FAILED (goal NOT reached) or CANCELLED
    Platform iterates over APPROVED contributions and issues a refund per
    contribution via MP's PaymentRefundClient. Money returns to the donor's
    original card. Status moves to COMPLETED (refund) or FAILED (manual
    review needed).

  Step 6 — Notifications
    The platform emails the donor at every relevant state change
    (APPROVED, REFUNDED, payout-completed-on-campaign) and the creator at
    state changes that affect them (new contribution, payout sent).
    These emails are informational confirmations only — they are NOT
    AFIP-valid comprobantes (see §7).

3. CUSTODY AND LEGAL FRAMING
================================================================================

The platform DOES NOT constitute a fideicomiso, neither per-campaign nor as
an umbrella trust. The legal framing is:

  - MercadoPago is the actual custodian of donor funds (and a PSP supervised
    by the BCRA). Funds reside in MP's books between donation and payout.
  - The platform is a technical intermediary that instructs MP when to hold,
    refund, or release.
  - This mirrors the Idea.me public model:
      "Once a project is submitted on Idea.me, the company enables the
       Idea.me account at MercadoPago to be custodian of the contributions
       until the campaign is finalized."

Implications:

  - No fideicomiso instrumentation, no fiduciario profesional, no escritura.
    Argument for the tesis panel: "delegamos la custodia en MercadoPago,
    PSP supervisado por el BCRA, evitando así constituir fideicomiso o
    cuenta segregada propia".
  - The platform is NOT an "oferta pública de valores" → no CNV registration
    required (the platform only handles donations / rewards, not equity or
    debt, so Ley 27.349 / Sección IX of the CNV PFC regime does NOT apply).
  - The platform is NOT a PSAV → no UIF Resolución 49/2024 obligations,
    because no crypto is handled.
  - The platform is NOT a PSP → no BCRA registration as PSPCP, because the
    platform itself never moves money outside MP rails.

Residual obligations the platform STILL has:

  - Ley 25.326 (Data Protection) applies to all personal data of donors and
    creators. Consent, ARCO rights, reasonable security, deletion on
    request, privacy policy. See creator-money-reception-requirements.txt
    §2.6 for the checklist.
  - Defensa del Consumidor (Ley 24.240) applies because the platform sells
    a service (intermediation) to consumers. Terms must be clear, accessible
    and not abusive.
  - Comercio electrónico best practices: precontractual information,
    transparency on fees, clear refund policy.

4. STAYING IN COLLEGE-PROJECT SCOPE
================================================================================

The tesis is academic, not a registered enterprise. To stay defensible:

  IN SCOPE (the platform DOES build):
    - Campaign creation, publication, browse, search.
    - Donor contribution flow via MP Payment Brick (cards only at MVP).
    - Idea.me-style hold-until-goal + payout / refund pipeline.
    - Creator verification wizard (per existing requirements doc).
    - Email notifications and on-screen confirmations.
    - Internal records of every transaction for audit traceability.
    - Basic admin panel to inspect contributions, payouts, refunds.

  OUT OF SCOPE (explicitly NOT built — document in tesis defense):
    - AFIP electronic invoice emission (WSFE / ARCA) on behalf of creators.
    - Acting as agente de retención / percepción for AFIP or ARBA.
    - Crypto / token contributions (would trigger PSAV regime).
    - Equity or loan crowdfunding (would trigger CNV PFC regime).
    - Formal UIF sujeto-obligado program (KYT, SAR reports, oficial de
      cumplimiento).
    - Multi-jurisdiction Ingresos Brutos handling.
    - International donors (would add IVA-servicios-digitales complexity).
    - Tokens, NFTs, smart contracts, blockchain custody.

  RATIONALE: Each "out of scope" item maps to a regulatory regime that
  would require a real legal entity, capital mínimo, oficiales designados,
  and recurring compliance cost. None of that is appropriate for a tesis.


================================================================================
5. DONOR SCOPE: CONSUMIDOR FINAL ONLY
================================================================================

Within the tesis scope, donors are ALWAYS treated as consumidor final.

  Implications and rationale:

  - The donation form asks for the minimum data: email, amount, optional
    reward selection, T&C acceptance. No CUIT, no razón social, no
    fiscal condition selector.
  - No factura is ever expected by the donor — they receive only an
    informational confirmation (see §7).
  - The "Necesito factura" branch described in earlier design notes is
    DEFERRED out of scope. If we ever wanted to support Monotributistas
    or RI donors, we would add an opt-in expander that collects CUIT +
    razón social and produces a voucher for the creator at payout time.
  - Data retention: donor records are kept for the campaign's lifetime
    plus a reasonable audit window (recommended 12 months), then deleted
    or anonymised per Ley 25.326's data-minimisation principle.

  Risk acknowledgment for the tesis defense:
    A real production platform receiving a donation above ARS 1.5M from a
    single donor would have UIF-PLD reporting obligations and should
    capture identifying data. For the academic prototype this threshold
    is irrelevant: test data only, no real money. Document this limit
    explicitly in the user-facing T&C: "los aportes están limitados a
    montos compatibles con el carácter académico del proyecto".


================================================================================
6. CREATOR SCOPE: ANY FISCAL CONDITION (and why it doesn't matter to us)
================================================================================

Creators can be any of: Consumidor Final, Monotributista, Responsable
Inscripto, Exento. They self-declare their condition in the verification
wizard.

  Why it does NOT matter to the platform:
    - The platform is not a fiscal agent for the creator.
    - The platform does not emit invoices on behalf of the creator.
    - The platform does not withhold taxes from the creator's payout.
    - Each creator handles their own AFIP obligations (Monotributo
      monthly, Ganancias annual, etc.) using their own systems.

  The two small caveats (still in scope to implement, minimally):
    1. Store the declared tax_condition on the creator record. This is
       captured for the creator's benefit (they can later download a
       payout report to feed their own bookkeeping) and to fulfil
       AFIP information-regime requests if they ever arrive — not for
       the platform's own tax computation.
    2. Make tax_condition mandatory before payout (not before publish).
       Without it, the platform cannot produce the payout report. It is
       NOT a blocker for receiving contributions — only for cashing out.

  What the platform DOES emit toward the creator:
    - At payout: a per-campaign report (PDF or CSV) listing gross,
      platform_fee, provider_fee, net, and date of transfer. This is an
      informational comprobante interno — NOT a Factura A/B/C.
    - The creator is responsible for issuing their own Factura
      (if applicable to their fiscal condition) through their own ARCA /
      AFIP Comprobantes en Línea — to the PLATFORM, for the income they
      received. This is outside the platform's workflow.

  What the platform receives FROM the creator: in production scale the
  creator would emit a Factura to the platform for the gross amount of
  donations (since legally the platform "buys" the right to intermediate).
  This invoice flow is also out of tesis scope. Tesis acknowledges the
  gap as a "production scaling consideration".

7. NOTIFICATIONS vs COMPROBANTES — what we issue and what we DON'T
================================================================================

You asked: "what we are giving the donors and creators are notifications
and confirmation that the transaction was made, no factura, no documento
contable — am I right?"

Answer: YES for the platform's own emissions. With one nuance about what
MercadoPago issues separately.

  What the platform issues:
    [x] On-screen confirmation in the UI after a successful donation
    [x] Email confirmation to the donor (amount, campaign, date,
        contribution ID, link to the campaign page)
    [x] Email notification to the donor on state changes (refund issued
        if campaign FAILED, payout completed if SUCCESSFUL)
    [x] Email notification to the creator on contribution APPROVED and
        payout completed
    [x] Internal log of every state transition (audit trail, not user-facing)

  What the platform does NOT issue:
    [ ] Factura A, B, C, M, X (no AFIP electronic invoice emission)
    [ ] Recibo oficial AFIP / comprobante fiscal
    [ ] Nota de débito / nota de crédito
    [ ] Certificado de retención
    [ ] Any document with CAE (Código de Autorización Electrónico)

  What MercadoPago DOES issue automatically (independent of the platform):
    - A receipt of the card charge to the donor's email (MP-branded,
      shows MP as merchant). This is MP's own comprobante, not AFIP-valid
      for IVA crédito-fiscal purposes from the donor's side.
    - A statement entry in the platform's MP account showing the credit.
    - On refund: an MP-side notification to the donor confirming reversal.
    - On payout / Money Out to the creator's CBU: an MP-side transfer
      record.

  Important framing for the tesis defense:
    The platform's confirmations are "comprobantes informativos" or
    "constancias de transacción" — they have legal weight as evidence
    that the operation occurred, but they are NOT documentos contables
    in the AFIP sense (no CAE, no IVA discrimination).

    If a creator or donor needs a true AFIP-valid factura, the emisor
    is whichever party is acting as vendor in their own fiscal context
    (typically the creator, if the contribution involved a reward) and
    they emit it through their own systems.

8. MERCADOPAGO INTEGRATION CHECKLIST
================================================================================

To keep the implementation tight and within scope:

  Required:
    [x] MP_ACCESS_TOKEN (server-side, TEST and PROD)
    [x] MP_PUBLIC_KEY  (client-side, for Payment Brick)
    [x] Payment Brick on the frontend for card input (no PCI scope)
    [x] /v1/payments call from backend with platform's access token
    [x] Webhook endpoint (/api/payments/notifications) for IPN
    [x] PaymentRefundClient for refund-on-failure flow
    [x] Payout to creator CBU on campaign success (TO IMPLEMENT — current
        code has this as TODO)
    [x] HTTPS everywhere (MP requires it for production credentials)
    [x] Idempotency keys on every /v1/payments and /v1/payouts call

  Discouraged for tesis scope:
    [ ] MP Marketplace OAuth (would change the model to Donacionar-style)
    [ ] Split payments / application_fee (same — different model)
    [ ] MP Subscriptions (recurring donations — out of scope MVP)
    [ ] Direct CBU transfers outside MP (would re-introduce custody risk)

  Operational hygiene:
    - Use a DEDICATED MercadoPago business account for the platform
      (never a personal account of any team member).
    - Withdraw the platform's own commission to a separate bank account
      from the third-party-funds account, to support the accounting
      separation argument.
    - Monitor MP dashboard for chargebacks / disputes weekly; build a
      simple admin endpoint to query MP balance vs sum of APPROVED
      contributions in non-paid-out campaigns. The numbers should match.

9. TESIS DEFENSE: HOW TO FRAME THE ARCHITECTURE
================================================================================

When the panel asks about the legal model, the recommended one-liner is:

  "ProyectoHornero implementa el modelo all-or-nothing tipo Idea.me. La
   plataforma actúa como intermediario técnico, delegando la custodia de
   fondos en MercadoPago — PSP supervisado por el BCRA — y por lo tanto
   no constituye fideicomiso ni cuenta segregada propia. No emite
   comprobantes fiscales: las facturas son responsabilidad del creador
   conforme su situación frente a AFIP. El alcance académico se limita
   a donantes consumidores finales sin emisión de factura electrónica,
   dejando la integración con ARCA/WSFE y el régimen UIF como
   consideraciones de escalamiento a producción."

Anticipate these likely panel questions:

  Q: ¿Qué pasa con la Ley 25.326?
  A: Compliance básico implementado: consentimiento informado, política
     de privacidad accesible, encriptación at rest de datos sensibles
     (DNI, CUIL/CUIT, CBU), derechos ARCO desde la configuración de
     cuenta, retención mínima necesaria, eliminación on-request.

  Q: ¿Por qué no se registran en CNV como PFC?
  A: La Sección IX del régimen CNV (PFC) regula crowdfunding de oferta
     pública de valores: equity y deuda. Donación / recompensa NO
     constituye oferta de valores y queda fuera del ámbito CNV.
     Ley 27.349 art. 22 lo confirma.

  Q: ¿Y el régimen UIF?
  A: La plataforma no es PSAV (no maneja cripto), no es PSP (delega en
     MP, que sí lo es), y no es entidad financiera. Por umbral de
     volumen y por naturaleza académica, no califica como sujeto
     obligado bajo Resolución UIF 49/2024.

  Q: ¿Quién emite la factura al donante?
  A: Dentro del alcance del proyecto (donantes consumidores finales,
     aportes pequeños, sin recompensa contractual obligatoria), no se
     emite factura. Si la operación incluye contraprestación, la
     emisión corresponde al creador conforme su condición frente a
     AFIP. La plataforma facilita los datos del donante en un reporte
     interno que el creador puede usar para emitir la factura desde
     sus propios sistemas.

  Q: ¿Y si una campaña fracasa?
  A: El flujo de refund automático devuelve cada contribución APPROVED
     a su tarjeta de origen vía PaymentRefundClient de MP. Esto está
     modelado en la tabla payments.refund y disparado por el cambio
     de estado de la campaña a FAILED o CANCELLED.

================================================================================
  REQUIREMENTS FOR ENABLING A USER TO RECEIVE MONEY FROM CAMPAIGNS
  Platform: ProyectoHornero (Argentina only, student-run project)
================================================================================

Based on: "Bootstrapping Competencia.pdf", "Investigacion temas impositivos.pdf",
and "Investigacion temas legales.pdf".

Since this is a student-run project (not a registered enterprise), it cannot act
as a formal financial intermediary, PSP, or PFC registered with the CNV. The
platform should operate as a simple intermediary/connector that facilitates
donations (not equity/lending), similar to Cafecito's model.

================================================================================
1. DATA TO ASK THE CREATOR (when enabling money reception)
================================================================================

--- Step 1: Basic Personal Information ---
  - Full legal name (Nombre y Apellido completo, as on DNI)
  - DNI number (Documento Nacional de Identidad)
  - CUIL number (Clave Única de Identificación Laboral) [11 digits, derived from DNI]
  - Date of birth
  - Email address (already have from registration)
  - Phone number (for contact/verification)
  - Address (province, city, street, zip code)
    → Province is needed to understand Ingresos Brutos jurisdiction

--- Step 2: Identity Verification (KYC - Know Your Customer) ---
  Required by UIF anti-money laundering obligations (even at minimal level):
  - Photo of DNI (front)
  - Photo of DNI (back)
  - Selfie holding the DNI (liveness check / proof the person is real)

  NOTE: Cafecito uses a third-party service (Galio Proof) for this. As students,
  you have simpler options:
    a) Manual review: Admin team reviews the uploaded photos visually.
    b) Use a free/low-cost identity verification API if budget allows.
  The key is to verify that the person is who they claim to be.

--- Step 3: Tax Situation (Situación Fiscal) ---
  - Tax condition (Condición ante AFIP):
      • Monotributista (category)
      • Responsable Inscripto
      • Consumidor Final / No inscripto
      • Exento
  - CUIT number (if Monotributista or Responsable Inscripto)
    [Same as CUIL for individuals, but confirms tax registration]

  WHY: The platform needs to know this because:
    - If the creator is Monotributista/Resp. Inscripto, they can issue invoices.
    - Donaciones puras (pure donations) have different tax treatment than
      rewards-based contributions.
    - The tax PDF states that if there's a contraprestación (reward), it's treated
      as a sale, not a donation, and triggers IVA obligations for the creator.

  NOTE FOR STUDENTS: You are NOT responsible for collecting taxes from creators.
  Each creator handles their own tax obligations. But you should inform them
  about their responsibilities and collect enough data to comply with potential
  AFIP information requests.

--- Step 4: Banking Information (for fund withdrawal) ---
  - CBU (Clave Bancaria Uniforme, 22 digits) — traditional bank account
    OR
  - CVU (Clave Virtual Uniforme, 22 digits) — virtual wallet (Mercado Pago, etc.)
  - Alias (optional, for human-readable reference)
  - Bank name or wallet provider name
  - Account holder name (must match the verified identity)

  NOTE: Following Cafecito's model, implement a "parking" period (e.g., 3-7 days)
  between receiving funds and allowing withdrawal, to handle disputes/fraud.

--- Step 5: Declaration / Terms Acceptance ---
  The creator must accept:
  - Terms and Conditions of the platform
  - That the information provided is truthful (Declaración Jurada)
  - That they are responsible for their own tax obligations
  - That the platform may retain a commission (specify %)
  - Privacy policy (Ley 25.326 compliance)
  - That the platform may share information with authorities if legally required
  - That campaign funds may be subject to a holding period before withdrawal


================================================================================
2. HOW TO STORE THIS INFORMATION
================================================================================

Given that you are students, not an enterprise, here are practical recommendations
that still respect the legal requirements (Ley 25.326 - Data Protection):

--- 2.1 Data Classification ---

  HIGHLY SENSITIVE (strongest protection):
    - DNI images (front/back)
    - Selfie with DNI
    - CBU/CVU
    - CUIT/CUIL number

  SENSITIVE (standard protection):
    - Full legal name
    - Address
    - Phone number
    - Tax condition

  BASIC (normal protection):
    - Email (already stored for auth)
    - Alias bancario
    - Accept/reject status of verification

--- 2.2 Database Storage (Backend - PostgreSQL/similar) ---

  Recommended tables/entities:

  TABLE: creator_verification
    - id (PK)
    - user_id (FK to users table)
    - full_legal_name (VARCHAR, encrypted at rest)
    - dni_number (VARCHAR, encrypted at rest)
    - cuil_number (VARCHAR, encrypted at rest)
    - cuit_number (VARCHAR, nullable, encrypted at rest)
    - date_of_birth (DATE)
    - phone_number (VARCHAR)
    - address_street (VARCHAR)
    - address_city (VARCHAR)
    - address_province (VARCHAR)
    - address_zip_code (VARCHAR)
    - tax_condition (ENUM: MONOTRIBUTISTA, RESPONSABLE_INSCRIPTO, CONSUMIDOR_FINAL, EXENTO)
    - verification_status (ENUM: PENDING, APPROVED, REJECTED)
    - rejection_reason (TEXT, nullable)
    - verified_at (TIMESTAMP, nullable)
    - verified_by (VARCHAR, nullable — which admin reviewed it)
    - created_at (TIMESTAMP)
    - updated_at (TIMESTAMP)

  TABLE: creator_bank_info
    - id (PK)
    - user_id (FK to users table)
    - account_type (ENUM: CBU, CVU)
    - account_number (VARCHAR, 22 chars, encrypted at rest)
    - account_alias (VARCHAR, nullable)
    - bank_or_wallet_name (VARCHAR)
    - account_holder_name (VARCHAR)
    - created_at (TIMESTAMP)
    - updated_at (TIMESTAMP)

  TABLE: creator_identity_documents
    - id (PK)
    - user_id (FK to users table)
    - document_type (ENUM: DNI_FRONT, DNI_BACK, SELFIE_WITH_DNI)
    - file_path (VARCHAR — reference to stored file, NOT the file itself in DB)
    - uploaded_at (TIMESTAMP)

--- 2.3 File Storage (DNI images / selfie) ---

  DO NOT store images directly in the database.

  Options (from simplest to most robust):
    a) Local encrypted filesystem:
       - Store in a dedicated folder outside the web-accessible directory
       - Path example: /secure-storage/identity-docs/{user_id}/
       - Set strict file permissions (chmod 600, owned by backend process only)
       - Name files with UUIDs, not original filenames

    b) Cloud storage with encryption (if using cloud):
       - S3 bucket (or similar) with:
         • Server-side encryption (SSE-S3 or SSE-KMS)
         • Bucket policy: private, no public access
         • Access only via signed URLs with short expiration (for admin review)

  IMPORTANT: Delete identity document images after verification is complete
  (or after a reasonable retention period, e.g., 6-12 months). Ley 25.326
  requires data minimization — don't keep what you don't need.

--- 2.4 Encryption Recommendations ---

  - At rest: Encrypt sensitive fields in the database.
    Practical approach for students:
      • Use application-level encryption (e.g., AES-256) for DNI, CUIL, CBU fields
      • Store the encryption key in an environment variable, NOT in the codebase
      • Or use database-level Transparent Data Encryption (TDE) if available

  - In transit: HTTPS everywhere (already standard with modern deployments)

  - Encryption key management:
      • Store keys in environment variables or a .env file (not committed to git)
      • For production: use a secrets manager if possible

--- 2.5 Access Control ---

  - Only admin users should be able to view identity documents and bank info
  - The creator themselves can view their own data (masked: show only last 4 digits
    of CBU/DNI in the UI)
  - Log every access to sensitive data (who viewed what and when)
  - Implement role-based access: ADMIN, CREATOR, DONOR

--- 2.6 Ley 25.326 Compliance Checklist ---

  As a student project, you won't register with the data protection authority,
  but you should still follow best practices:

  [x] Inform users what data you collect and why (Privacy Policy)
  [x] Get explicit consent before collecting identity data
  [x] Allow users to request access to their data (derecho de acceso)
  [x] Allow users to request rectification of incorrect data
  [x] Allow users to request deletion of their data (derecho de supresión)
  [x] Implement reasonable security measures (encryption, access control)
  [x] Don't share data with third parties without consent
  [x] Delete data when it's no longer needed

3. RECOMMENDED USER FLOW (SUMMARY)
================================================================================

  1. User registers on the platform (basic email/password or OAuth)
  2. User clicks "Become a Creator" or "Enable Money Reception"
  3. Step 1: Fill personal data form (name, DNI, CUIL, address, phone, DOB)
  4. Step 2: Upload identity documents (DNI front, DNI back, selfie with DNI)
  5. Step 3: Declare tax situation (tax condition, CUIT if applicable)
  6. Step 4: Enter banking info (CBU or CVU, bank name, holder name)
  7. Step 5: Accept terms, declaration, and privacy policy
  8. Status → PENDING VERIFICATION
  9. Admin reviews identity documents + data consistency
  10. Status → APPROVED or REJECTED (with reason)
  11. If approved → Creator can now create campaigns and withdraw funds
  12. Withdrawals have a 3-7 day parking/holding period

================================================================================
4. WHAT YOU CAN SKIP AS A STUDENT PROJECT (pragmatic notes)
================================================================================

  - UIF formal registration as a sujeto obligado (you're not a real financial entity)
  - CNV registration (you're doing donations/rewards, not equity crowdfunding)
  - Formal PSAV registration (you're not handling crypto)
  - Automated facial recognition / biometric liveness detection
    (manual admin review of photos is sufficient for a university project)
  - Acting as agente de retención for AFIP (not applicable for students)
  - Ingresos Brutos provincial registration

  HOWEVER, you should still:
  - Implement basic KYC (document upload + manual review)
  - Segregate platform funds from creator funds conceptually
  - Have clear terms & conditions
  - Encrypt sensitive data
  - Follow data protection best practices (Ley 25.326)
  - Inform creators about their tax responsibilities
